protein shell around a central cavity in which the genome re-

network of protein-protein contacts on the inside of the capsid,
which are particularly extensive about the fivefold axes (Fig.
4.13C). The interaction of five VP1 molecules, which is unique
to the fivefold axes, results in a prominent protrusion extend-
ing to about 25 A from the capsid shell (Fig. 4.13A). The pro-
trusion appears as a steep-walled plateau encircled by a valley
or cleft. In the capsids of many picornaviruses, these depres-
sions, which may contain the receptor-binding sites, are so
deep that they have been termed canyons.

One of several important lessons learned from high-
