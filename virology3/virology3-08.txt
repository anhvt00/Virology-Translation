(97-amino-acid) protein is a minor component of virus parti-

associate to form a noncovalent tetramer that functions as an
ion channel. This viral ion channel is the target of the influenza
virus inhibitor drug amantadine (Volume II, Fig. 9.13). The
effects of this drug, as well as of mutations in the M2 coding
sequence, indicate that M2 plays important roles during both

(Chapter 13). M2 belongs to a class of channel-creating viral
proteins called viroporins, which are present in a number of
other enveloped viruses, such as hepatitis C virus and Sindbis
virus, but also in nonenveloped viruses like simian virus 40
and papillomaviruses.
